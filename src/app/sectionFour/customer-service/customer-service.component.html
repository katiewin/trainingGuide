Front Desk Etiquette and Handling Stressful Situations

As a front desk attendant, your role is crucial in creating a positive and welcoming environment for patrons. Here are some general tips to keep in mind for daily interactions, along with guidance on handling more stressful situations.

General Tips:

  • Always be aware when you are sitting at the front desk.
  • When someone comes in, look up from what you are doing and acknowledge with a nod, smile, or hello.
  • Sit straight in your chair at all times. You must remain professional and be mindful of people watching you
  • NO headphones can be used at the desk EVER
  • Make eye contact when someone is asking a question.
  • Don't be afraid to say, "I don't know."
  • Never give out private information to people who aren't the patron, for example, a student's parent.
  • Be discreet about what the patrons are checking out; never comment on it and try not to say the title out loud.

Handling Stressful Situations:

If a patron is angry, it is essential to stay calm. Say something like, "I really want to help. Thank you for bearing with me as I do this for you."

If a patron is demanding something you can't do, make it known and, if possible, direct them to someone who can. Say something like, "Unfortunately, I'm unable to fulfill this request, but let me bring in my manager who may be able to help more."

If you feel uncomfortable in any way, it's okay to come and get someone else as backup. For example, if a patron is getting too close or making inappropriate comments, you can say, "I have something urgent I need to take care of, but [colleague's name] can help you."

It's also appropriate to firmly tell someone, "This is inappropriate. Please stop."

Never give out personal information:

If someone has a question, take down their information and have someone get back to them.
